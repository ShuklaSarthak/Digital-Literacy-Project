Stay Safe Online - Checklist (For College Students)

As college students, we're constantly online-for classes, payments, social media, and more. Here's a simple checklist to stay safe and avoid common cyber scams:
  
  Do's
* Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
* Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA) on apps like email, banking, and social media.
* Always verify UPI payment requests before approving-double-check the name and amount.
* Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone-even if they claim to be from your bank.
* Check website URLs before entering personal info (look for "https://" and avoid suspicious links).
* Download apps only from official stores like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
* Log out from shared/public computers (like in college labs or cyber cafťs).
* Keep your social media accounts private and avoid oversharing personal details (like phone number, address, college ID).

  Don'ts
* Don't click on random links or attachments from unknown emails or WhatsApp messages.
* Don't scan unknown QR codes-this can lead to unauthorized payments.
* Don't trust strangers offering "easy money," internships, or giveaways online.
* Don't use public Wi-Fi for banking or UPI transactions.
* Don't ignore sudden "urgent" payment requests-they are often scams.

  If You Become a Victim:
* Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
* Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (quick action can help recover money)

  Staying alert online is just as important as staying safe offline-one small mistake can cost a lot!
