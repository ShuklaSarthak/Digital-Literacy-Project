# Case Study on Phishing
Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers trick people into sharing sensitive information—like passwords, bank details, or OTPs—by pretending to be a trusted source. These messages usually come as emails, SMS, or chats that look like they’re from banks, companies, or even college portals. The main idea is simple: create urgency and panic so the person acts quickly without verifying. <br>

A typical phishing attack follows a few clear steps. First, the attacker gathers basic details about the target (like email or workplace). Next, they send a fake message that looks genuine—for example, claiming your account is blocked or there’s suspicious activity. The message includes a link that looks real but leads to a fake website. When the victim clicks it and enters their login details or OTP, the attacker instantly captures that information. In some cases, malware may also get installed. <br>

A real example is the 2016 FACC “fake president” scam, where attackers impersonated the CEO and tricked an employee into transferring millions of euros to a fake account. This shows how even professionals can fall for well-crafted phishing attempts. <br>

Phishing targets everyone—students, employees, and even large organizations. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and data breaches. In companies, it can damage reputation and cause huge financial setbacks. Overall, phishing highlights the importance of staying alert and always verifying messages before taking action online.
